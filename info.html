Access to Nepal

By Air
Nepal has only one international airport lies in Kathmandu. The important airlines that serve Kathmandu are Indian Airlines, Thai Airways International, Bimal Bangladesh, GMG Airlines, China Southern Airlines, Druk Air, Qatar Airways, Pakistan International Airlines, Gulf Air, Sahara Ailrlines, Jet Air, Kingfisher Airlines,Etihad,Silk Air,Korean Air, Jetlite,Jet Airways,Indian Airlines, Dragon Air, Baharine, Air China, Air Arabia, Air Asia, Malaysian Air and Oman Air(Muscat)

The National Carrier
Nepal Airlines Corporation (NAC) is the national flag carrier of Nepal with flights to/from Delhi, Dubai, Doha, Bangkok, Hongkong and Kuala Lumpur.

Other International Airlines operating from/to Indian Airlines, Thai Airways International, Bimal Bangladesh, GMG Airlines,China Southern Airlines, Druk Air, Qatar Airways, Pakistan International Airlines, Gulf Air, Sahara Airlines, Jet Air, Kingfisher Airlines, Etihad, Silk Air, Korean Air,Jetlite, Jet Airways,Indian Airlines, Dragon Air, Baharine, Air China, Air Arabia, Arkefly and Oman Air(Muscat).

Via Asia
You could travel to Kathmandu via Bangkok, Hong Kong and Singapore. There are daily flights to Kathmandu from Bangkok.If in India, you can fly to Nepal from Delhi, Mumbai, Calcutta and Varanasi. There is the spectacular flight from Lhasa to Kathmandu on Saturdays and Tuesdays operated by China Southwest Airlines. You can also fly Druk Air from Paro in Bhutan, or take a flight from Dhaka, Bangladesh.

From Europe and Middle East
Qatar Airways and Gulf Air operates daily flights to Kathmandu from Doha and Dubai.

From North America
You could fly to Nepal via India or alternatively via Bangkok or Hong Kong. Thai Airways, Qatar Airways, Gulf Air flight makes good connections with North American flights.

From Australia and New Zealand
Look for routes via Singapore, Hong Kong, Bangkok or Malaysia

By Over Land
Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal is connected with India through the fertile plains of the Terai by the most picturesque highway. Facilities of exchange are available at the point of entry. There are regular day and night bus services from Kathmandu to all the major entry and exit points. The bus fare is comparatively cheap. There are just ten entry points into Nepal by land which  are : Kakarvitta, Birgunj, Belhiya, Sunauli, Nepalgunj, Dhangadi, Mahendranagar.The Arniko Highway (Kodari - Kathmandu) links Kathmandu with the Tibet Region of the People?s Republic of China to the north east Nepal.
